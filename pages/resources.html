Crop-Specific Fertilizer Applications

Corn (Maize) - MAIS Application Guide

Fertilizer Program for Optimal Corn Yield

Base Fertilizer Application: Apply Complete Fertilizer (NPK 14-14-14) or Ammonium Phosphate (16-20-0) mixed into soil as basal fertilizer before planting or during planting operation.

Top Dressing: Apply Urea (46-0-0) to soil away from plant roots or after 15 days from transplanting.

  • Timing: Basal application at planting; topdressing at 15 days
  • Recommended Product: NPK 14-14-14 or 16-20-0
  • Benefits: Increased yields and better quality harvest
  • Application Rate: Follow label recommendations based on soil analysis

Rice (Palay) - PALAY Application Guide

Fertilizer Program for Maximum Rice Production

Base Fertilizer Application: Mix Complete Fertilizer (NPK 14-14-14) or Ammonium Phosphate (16-20-0) into soil as basal fertilizer before planting.

Top Dressing: Apply Urea (46-0-0) or broadcast over soil before panicle initiation starts OR apply after 45 days from transplanting.

  • Timing: Basal at planting; topdressing at panicle initiation or 45 days
  • Recommended Products: NPK 14-14-14 or 16-20-0
  • Benefits: Higher grain quality and increased yield
  • Critical Phase: Nutrient timing during panicle development

Understanding Fertilizer Nutrients

Primary Plant Nutrients and Their Functions

Most agricultural fertilizers contain three primary plant nutrients essential for growth and production:

  • Nitrogen (N): Promotes leaf growth and green foliage development
  • Phosphorus (P): Supports root development and flower/fruit formation
  • Potassium (K): Strengthens plant structure and disease resistance

Each fertilizer product contains different proportions of these nutrients, formulated for specific crop needs and growth stages.

Fertilizer Product Composition

Understanding NPK Ratios and Formulations

Our product line offers various combinations of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium to meet different agricultural needs:

  • NPK 14-14-14 - Complete balanced formula for most crops
  • NPK 16-20-0 - High in nitrogen and phosphorus for specific crops
  • UREA 46-0-0 - High nitrogen concentrate for leafy growth
  • AS 21-0-0+24S - Nitrogen with sulfur for field applications

Each formulation is designed to address specific nutrient requirements at different crop growth stages.

Best Practices for Fertilizer Application

Measurement and Application Techniques

Proper Handling and Application Methods

  • Measure fertilizer accurately according to recommendations
  • Mix thoroughly before application for even distribution
  • Apply during morning or late afternoon for best results
  • Ensure soil moisture is adequate for nutrient uptake
  • Avoid applying fertilizer directly on plant leaves
  • Follow crop-specific timing and application guidelines

Safety and Environmental Care

Safety Precautions and Responsibility

  • Always wear protective equipment when handling fertilizers
  • Store fertilizers in cool, dry locations
  • Keep away from moisture, heat, and direct sunlight
  • Read and follow all label instructions before use
  • Dispose of empty containers responsibly
  • Maintain proper ventilation during storage and handling
  • Keep out of reach of children and livestock

Soil Health and Sustainability

Long-term Agricultural Productivity

Proper fertilizer management is essential for maintaining soil health and sustainable agricultural productivity. Regular soil testing helps determine nutrient needs and optimize fertilizer application. Combined with good farming practices, our products help build healthy soils that support productive crops for years to come.

  • Conduct soil analysis before each growing season
  • Follow recommended application rates and schedules
  • Rotate crops to maintain soil fertility
  • Combine with organic matter for improved soil structure
  • Monitor plant growth and adjust applications as needed
